TaskFlow Project Charter
1. Problem StatementProblem 1: Employees have no single, reliable place to see all their task deadlines — they rely on scattered emails, WhatsApp messages, and manual reminders.Why it matters: Deadlines are missed regularly because notifications get buried or forgotten. This erodes client trust, triggers penalty clauses, and forces last-minute firefighting that burns out the team.Problem 2: Managers cannot see real-time progress across projects — they only find out about delays after they've already happened, often through verbal updates or chasing individuals.Why it matters: Without visibility, managers cannot reallocate resources, unblock stuck tasks, or adjust priorities proactively. Small delays compound into major schedule overruns, and leadership makes decisions based on gut feeling rather than facts — increasing the risk of project failure.Problem 3: Project communication is split across emails, WhatsApp groups, and Excel files — information is fragmented, decisions are lost in long threads, and team members miss updates because they weren't copied.Why it matters: Misalignment becomes the norm: two people work on conflicting versions of a task, questions get re-asked wasting hours, and there's no audit trail of why decisions were made. This kills productivity, breeds frustration, and makes onboarding new members nearly impossible.Problem 4: Generating status reports requires manually consolidating data from multiple spreadsheets, email chains, and chat histories — taking managers hours each week.Why it matters: Reporting becomes a low-value, high-cost chore. Reports are always outdated by the time they're shared, so stakeholders get stale information. Managers spend more time documenting work than doing work, and the delay in reporting means critical issues are flagged too late to fix without extra cost.Overarching Root CauseThe company relies on disconnected, manual tools that do not scale with team growth. Operational complexity increases significantly as the number of projects, employees, and communication channels grows. The overhead from coordination and error-correction eats into margins, client satisfaction drops, and the team loses confidence in leadership's ability to deliver consistently.2. Project Scope2.1 Product IdentifierProject Name: TaskFlowVersion: 1.0Description: A centralized, web-based system designed to consolidate task and project information into a single platform, enabling transparent tracking, efficient collaboration, and instant reporting.2.2 In-Scope (High-Level Features)User & Team Management: Create, edit, and deactivate user accounts; assign users to specific teams. (Note: Specific user roles and permissions will be defined during the requirements analysis phase).Project Management: Create, edit, and close projects; assign dedicated project managers; set project timelines and descriptions.Task Management: Create and assign tasks within projects; set task priorities, tracking statuses (e.g., To Do, In Progress, Done), and due dates.Real-time Dashboard: Provide managers with a visual overview of key project metrics, progress tracking, and upcoming deadlines.Collaboration & Notifications: Implement a central notification system for task assignments, status updates, and approaching deadlines alongside task-level commenting threads.Reporting: Automated generation of project status reports and individual performance summaries to track completed versus overdue tasks.Access Security: Enforce strong user authentication and role-based access control to protect company data and prevent unauthorized system access.2.3 Out-of-Scope (Exclusions)Native mobile applications for iOS and Android (only responsive web design is included in this release).Automatic data migration or scraping from existing legacy Excel spreadsheets or WhatsApp histories.Integration with third-party external tools (such as Slack, Jira, or external calendars).Advanced HR modules like time tracking, employee timesheets, or client billing software.Direct client or external guest access to project dashboards.Automated workload balancing or algorithmic resource management.Custom UI white-labeling or unique branding per department beyond the core company logo and color scheme.3. Constraints & Assumptions3.1 ConstraintsThe application must be accessible via modern web browsers across desktop, tablet, and mobile interfaces.The application shall support the latest two major versions of modern web browsers.Initial user capacity limits will be determined during the system design phase based on expected company growth projections.The user interface must adhere to modern usability standards to ensure a clean, intuitive, and highly responsive user experience.3.2 AssumptionsUsers will have active, valid company email addresses prior to account creation.The client will designate authorized administrators to manage user onboarding and role configuration.The organization's staff will have stable internet access during operational hours to utilize the platform.The client will provide timely feedback during requirement validation and project review meetings.Core business workflows must be formally approved by management before software implementation begins.4. Success CriteriaReduced Turnaround Friction: Project managers must be able to view the real-time status of any project instantly on the dashboard without relying on manual follow-ups or external spreadsheets.Reporting Efficiency: The time required to generate a comprehensive project status report must be reduced from hours of manual gathering to a single, on-demand system action.Improved Deadline Adherence: Achieve a measurable reduction in overdue tasks compared to the current manual process baseline within the first three months of active platform deployment.High Active Engagement: At least 80% of active employees must log in and use the system weekly within two months of deployment.
